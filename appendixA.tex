\chapter{Full Candidate Software List}
\label{appendix:full-candidate-software-list}

This appendix records the candidate software packages considered during candidate discovery (Section~\ref{sec:candidate-software-discovery}). The list combines packages surfaced through academic survey papers, community-curated lists, and LLM-assisted discovery. Each package was scored by mention count, then evaluated against the filtering criteria in Section~\ref{sec:filtering-criteria}. The ranked mention counts are preserved in \path{examples/software-list-counting-result.xlsx} (sheet \texttt{final.m}); the source catalogue and archived LLM-assisted outputs are in \path{examples/sofware-counting-webs.csv}.

Table~\ref{tab:full-candidate-list} lists the candidate packages with their primary role in the MPS workflow and their status after filtering. Packages marked \textit{Measured} are reported in Chapter~\ref{ch:measurement-results}; packages marked \textit{Excluded} are explained in Appendix~\ref{appendix:excluded-software-packages}; packages marked \textit{Not selected} appeared in one or more discovery sources but did not reach the measured set, either because they ranked below the cutoff after mention counting and filtering, or because they are subsumed by a measured package (for example, \textit{Orocos~KDL} is a component of \textit{OROCOS}).

The 28 measured packages were selected after the mention-count ranking and the filtering criteria in Section~\ref{sec:filtering-criteria}. Packages marked \textit{Not selected} appeared in the candidate pool but did not meet the open-source, repository-measurability, or activity criteria, or were subsumed by another measured package. Packages such as \textit{KineoWorks}, \textit{Robotics Toolbox for MATLAB}, \textit{RoboDK}, \textit{RobotStudio}, and \textit{Delmia} are proprietary or industrial tools and therefore fall outside the open-source criterion. \textit{ROS}~(ROS~1) is the predecessor of \textit{ROS~2}; the active successor was chosen so that the measurement reflects current practice.
